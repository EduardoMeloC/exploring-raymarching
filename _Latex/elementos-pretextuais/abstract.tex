This work aims to explore Ray Marching, a less popular cousin of Ray Tracing that still finds relevant use cases to this day. We showcase its strenghts as we put together an implementation of clouds, explaining the powerful tools the method has to offer. To lay the foundations of the method, we take apart Signed Distance Functions (SDFs), as they allow for the smooth shape modeling required for organic clouds, and are difficult to achieve with regular polygons. We also present a Ray Marching implementation in GLSL and directly compare its characteristics with Ray Tracing, showcasing what differences there are in performance, fidelity, and practical use cases.
